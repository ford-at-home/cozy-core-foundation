# Tighten

Questions whose answers would let the next pass be tighter.

- **Is the opening Discord scene yours?** It's written as lived observation
  ("a server you joined for reasons you've forgotten") because the voice
  calls for a scene, but it is a composite, not a documented event. If you
  have a real instance — a real brand, a real server — the whole piece gets
  a spine. If not, consider signaling the composite more openly.
- **How much does the persona already know?** The piece re-derives the
  Tier 1 / Tier 3 logic from the memo. If your reader has genuinely
  internalized the memo, the "Two doors closed at once" section could
  compress to a paragraph and the piece could spend that space on the ethics
  section, which is currently the thinnest load-bearing part.
- **The "wince, priced" section quotes two metrics (48-hour slang decay,
  92% F-1).** Is two enough, or does the section want the full absurdist
  list? There's a version of this piece that leans harder on the comedy of
  putting SLAs on embarrassment.
- **The closer implicates the market's limits rather than the reader.** It's
  quiet, per the voice. But test whether "That's the market working" reads
  as resigned rather than pointed — the line is doing reframe work and needs
  to land as observation, not shrug.
- **Section headers.** "Where I stop nodding" is doing the implication work;
  confirm the other three headers earn their casualness or whether the piece
  reads better with fewer breaks at longform length.

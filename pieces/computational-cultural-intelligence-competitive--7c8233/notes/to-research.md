# To research

Gaps where the piece would be stronger with a primary source. The research memo
asserts these; it does not link them to a verifiable origin.

- **The 20–40% decline in public forum engagement.** The memo states it as
  fact with no attached citation. The nearest linked source is
  [Pulsar's identity-communities piece](https://www.pulsarplatform.com/blog/2026/identity-behavior-organizes-online-community-demographics),
  which covers the reorganization but not that specific range. Find the
  underlying study or soften the number.
- **Discord niche-community mentions at "nearly triple" the volume of public
  forums.** Marked `[Source needed]` inline in the proposal. The memo calls
  this "platform telemetry" without naming the platform or the measurement
  window. Where to look: Pulsar TRAC's own published dashboards, or the
  Pulsar blog series the memo already cites.
- **"Over half of marketers spend under 30 minutes vetting creators."** The
  memo's citation trail suggests this comes from
  [influData's brand-safety material](https://infludata.com/brand-safety-analysis)
  or [Strategic Media's brand-safety piece](https://www.strategicmediainc.com/newsroom/brand-safety-in-the-age-of-ai/),
  but neither is pinned to the stat in the memo text. Confirm before this
  number survives to a final draft.
- **Search/platform penalties for AI-sounding text.** The memo asserts
  algorithms "penalize synthetic-sounding content." The piece hedges this to
  the memo's own claim, but a primary source (e.g., Google's spam policy
  updates on scaled content abuse) would let the piece say it flatly.
- **The memo's Tier assignments are single-analyst grades.** No methodology
  is given for how the thirteen criteria roll up into tiers. If this piece is
  used to argue an actual investment decision, the persona will want the
  scoring rubric or at least a second analyst pass.
- **Concept 12's tier is missing from the memo's own comparison table** (the
  row ends after the pricing column). The piece deliberately assigns it no
  tier; confirm the intended grade with the memo's author before a final
  draft leans on it.

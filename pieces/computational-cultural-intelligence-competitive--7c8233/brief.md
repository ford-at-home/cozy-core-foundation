## Persona

A founder-operator with one bet to place. She's been circling the "cultural
intelligence" space for months — she has the twelve-concept landscape memo
open in one tab and her runway spreadsheet in another. She doesn't need
another taxonomy; she needs to know which of these ideas are businesses,
which are features a foundation model absorbs next year, and what the moat
actually is made of.

## Throughline

The defensible businesses in this landscape aren't the ones that detect
machines — they're the ones that hold access to the private rooms culture
retreated into, and the judgment to know what plays there.

## Why this persona, why now

The research describes a market being formed by two forces moving at once:
public forums are emptying out as AI absorbs their utility value, and public
channels are flooding with synthetic boilerplate that platforms now penalize.
Every one of the twelve product concepts is a different wager on how brands
buy back the cultural fluency those shifts took from them. The persona is
deciding right now where to put her next two years, and the loudest option —
generic AI-text detection — is precisely the one the research grades as a
trap. If this piece lands, she stops evaluating these concepts as a feature
list and starts evaluating them as claims on access and judgment, which is
what the Tier 1 / Tier 3 split in the research actually encodes.

# Brief: standardized-protocols-for-agentic-ai-b89998

## Voice

inline (from profile)

## Persona

A technical reader who understands LLM agents at a systems level but needs a clean map of the emerging protocol stack before making architecture, integration, or vendor decisions.

## Throughline

Agentic AI protocols are not one standards race; they are a layered stack where MCP handles tools, A2A handles agent handoffs, decentralized alternatives fill narrower trust gaps, and security remains the part that has not caught up.

## Channels

- longform

## Why this persona, why now

The agent ecosystem has moved quickly enough that a technical reader can easily mistake protocol announcements for interchangeable standards. The useful move now is to separate layers: tool invocation, agent-to-agent task exchange, identity, discovery, transport, and governance. That lets the reader evaluate MCP, A2A, ANP, AGNTCY, vendor tools, and standards-body work without treating every launch as a replacement for every other launch.

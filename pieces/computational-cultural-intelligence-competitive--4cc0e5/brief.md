## Persona

A founder-investor hybrid — someone with the twelve "computational cultural intelligence" product concepts on their desk and real money to allocate — who has read enough market maps to distrust them, and needs to know which concepts are fundable businesses and which are features a generalist model eats next quarter.

## Throughline

The winners in cultural intelligence aren't the tools that understand culture best — they're the tools that sell fear to buyers with risk budgets and sit on data nobody else can reach.

## Why this persona, why now

Two structural shifts are happening at once and neither waits for a second fundraising cycle: consumers are abandoning public forums for closed identity communities, and AI-generated boilerplate has made "sounding human" a measurable economic asset. Every one of the twelve concepts is a bet on some slice of those shifts, but the research shows the tiers separate on two unglamorous variables — whose budget pays and whether the data is reachable — not on how clever the cultural analysis is. If this reader allocates by demo-appeal instead of by budget-and-moat, they fund the Tier 3 concepts that look most impressive and get displaced by Sprinklr or a generalist model within a year. The window on the actual moat — compliant access to closed platforms — is closing as those platforms tighten API terms.
